# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want CP Sidekick to be a welcoming project for people learning and practicing competitive programming. We expect contributors and maintainers to be respectful, constructive, and patient.

## Expected Behavior

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Be respectful of different experience levels.
- Give feedback that helps people improve.
- Assume good intent, and ask clarifying questions when something is unclear.
- Keep discussion focused on the project and its learning-first mission.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks.
- Dismissive comments about someone's background, skill level, or identity.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Encouraging cheating, contest rule violations, or misuse of the extension.
- Repeated disruptive behavior after maintainers ask for it to stop.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, or restrict participation when behavior harms the project community.

If you see a problem, report it through the project's GitHub issue tracker or by contacting a maintainer through the repository's listed contact method.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, and any official community channels.
